Welcome participants!

Please find a list of questions that we are frequently asked. Regarding Course Content

1. What is Free Software?

Free Software is software which ensure that they provide the necessary freedom to the users, mainlythe freedom to use, study, modify and share both the original software and the modifications. Good examples of these are operating systems like Ubuntu, Fedora or application softwares like VLC and Mozilla Firefox. On similar lines languages like C, C++, Java, Python, Perl, Ruby and frameworks like Qt, Django, Ruby on Rails follow the popular free software development methodology ensuring that the languages and the necessary compilers, development kits are all under free software licenses. We at FSMK, try to promote these technologies and ensure that information and knowledge of such free software reach to students. More info here.

2. Will attending such camp and learning free software technologies help me in placements?

As per a GARTNER report, 90% of Indian IT organizations will use Free and open source software for their main projects by 2016. There is already a growing trend in the industry to prefer equivalent free software over proprietary technologies. Hence learning free software technologies will definitely help students for their career in the software field.

3. Will you share all the software used during the camp so that I can use it after the camp?

Workshop kit will contain a customized Ubuntu DVD which will have all the necessary software used during the camp. The license of free software encourages users to share the software with others and spread the word. Infact, after the camp, we would also encourage you to go back to your college and share the software and course material with your friends.

4. Can I look at the previous course material?

The previous year's course material is shared at FSMK's github profile here and here. Unfortunately we didn't do a very good job at collecting the material from speakers last time and hence you may not get all the material. Based on the past experience, we have learnt a lot and will definitely do better this time in ensuring that all materials by resource persons are collected and distributed amongst students.Regarding Registration

5. I have registered, where should I pay the registration fees?

After registration, you should have got an email to the registered email address regarding the payment of registration fees. Please follow the instruction as specified in it. However, in general, if you already have a FSMK GNU/Linux User's Group(GLUG) in your college, then do contact the core members of the GLUG for payment of registration fees. If not, someone from FSMK will contact you regarding the registration fees.

6. I have paid the registration fees, but I will not able to attend the camp. Can you refund the registration fees?

Sorry, we have strict policy of no refunds. As we make necessary arrangements for the camp based on the number of people who have registered. We are sorry to hear that you will not be able to attend the camp and we would encourage you to find someone who has not registered yet and who is interested to attend the camp in place of you.

7. If it is about Free Software, why are you charging money?

Glad that you have this question. If you have already participated in any events associated with FSMK, there is a good chance that it was a free event. FSMK is a non-profit registered organization with everybody working for it as volunteers. Apart from raising funds through regular college activities or supporting other organizations to move to free software, such events are one of our main funding sources. Though most of the registration fees is spent for the organizing the camp, mainly in food cost and for workshop kit, we do plan to save some money taken from you as part of registration fees for future FSMK activities. However we do arrive at registration fees after getting inputs from students in various colleges, ensuring that the registration fees doesnt become the main reason for a student for not participating in the camp. For students coming from economic weaker sections or students from other marginal sections, we do plan to give scholarships. Please do go through this link for more information about scholarships.

8. I want to attend only few days of camp, can I do that?

Sorry, since we have limited number of seats for the camp, we would like to entertain participants only who can be available throughout the duration of the camp. This will ensure that the participants can make best use of the camp. Hence, if you are registering, please ensure that you are available for the entire duration of the camp.

9. What are the items to be brought to the camp?

  • laptops if you have
  • pen drives
  • wifi dongles if you have
  • Notebook and pen

10.Will the camp be for localites only?

No, but since this is a regional camp, no accommodation facilities will be provided by the host.
